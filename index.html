AYODHYA is a city situated on the banks of the Sarayu river in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is the administrative headquarters of the Ayodhya district as well as the Ayodhya division of Uttar Pradesh, India.

Ayodhya, town in northern India that is known as a sacred city and the birthplace of Rama in Hinduism and as a historical center of Buddhism. On a site significant to both Hindus and Muslims was a Mughal-era mosque, the Babri Masjid, which was destroyed in 1992 amid interreligious tensions. After a 2019 Supreme Court verdict granting the site to Hindus, a new Ram temple is under construction, with an inauguration date of January 22, 2024. Ayodhya lies on the Ghaghara River, also known as Sarayu or Sarju in the lower course, just east of Faizabad in south-central Uttar Pradesh state.
An ancient town, Ayodhya is regarded as one of the seven sacred cities of the Hindus, revered because of its association in the great Indian epic poem Ramayana with the birth of Rama and with the rule of his father, Dasharatha. MYTHOLOGY

The Ramayana( Sanskrit: रामायणम्, romanized: Rāmāyaṇa) also known as Valmiki Ramayana, as traditionally attributed to Valmiki, is a smriti text (also described as an Sanskrit epic) from ancient India, one of the two important epics of Hinduism known as the Itihasas, the other being the Mahabharata. The epic narrates the life of Rama, a prince of Ayodhya in the kingdom of Kosala. The epic follows his fourteen-year exile to the forest urged by his father King Dasharatha, on the request of Rama's stepmother Kaikeyi; his travels across forests in the Indian subcontinent with his wife Sita and brother Lakshmana; the kidnapping of Sita by Ravana, the king of Lanka, that resulted in war; and Rama's eventual return to Ayodhya along with Sita to be crowned king amidst jubilation and celebration.

HISTORY

Ayodhya, located in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, is one of the oldest cities in India and holds immense religious significance in Hinduism. It is believed to be the birthplace of Lord Rama, a major deity in Hinduism, and the setting of the ancient epic Ramayana. Ayodhya has been a center of pilgrimage for Hindus for centuries. In recent history, Ayodhya gained prominence due to the Babri Masjid-Ram Janmabhoomi dispute, a socio-political conflict over a religious site in the city. This conflict culminated in the demolition of the Babri Masjid in 1992, leading to significant communal tensions and legal battles. The site is now slated for the construction of a Ram Temple following a Supreme Court verdict in 2019. Today, Ayodhya remains an important cultural and religious center in India.

DISPUTE

The Ayodhya dispute is a complex social, religious, historical, and political debate in India revolving around a land in Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh. The conflict dates back over 500 years and has seen various legal and historical developments, culminating in a landmark Supreme Court verdict on November 9, 2019.
The demolition of the mosque resulted in several months of inter-communal violence between India's Hindu and Muslim communities, causing the death of an estimated 2,000 people in Bombay (now Mumbai) as a direct consequence, and triggering riots all over the Indian subcontinent. A day after the demolition of the mosque, on 7 December 1992, The New York Times reported that over 30 Hindu temples across Pakistan were attacked, some set on fire, and one was demolished. Hindu temples in Bangladesh were also attacked.

CONSTRUCTION OF TEMPLE

Ayodhya is a city in Uttar Pradesh, India, that is believed to be the birthplace of the Hindu deity Lord Rama. The construction of the temple dedicated to him is a complex issue that has been ongoing for several centuries, with many disputes and conflicts arising over the years. The temple construction officially started again after a Bhumi-Pujan (transl. ground breaking ceremony) on 5 August 2020. The three-day long Vedic ritual was held ahead of the groundbreaking ceremony, which revolved around the installation of a 40 kg (88 pounds) silver brick as the foundation stone by Prime Minister Narendra Modi.On the day before on 4 August, the Ramarchan Puja (transl. Puja of Rama's feet) was performed, in order to ritually invite all the major deities into the temple.

PRESENT SITUATION OF AYODHYA

Ayodhya is a city in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. The current situation in Ayodhya is peaceful, with no major incidents reported recently. The city has seen significant development in recent years, with the construction of new infrastructure, including a smart city project and a new airport. The city is also home to several historical and religious sites, including the Ram temple, which is a major pilgrimage destination for Hindus.
In January 2022, Ayodhya continues to be a significant religious and cultural center in India. Following the Supreme Court verdict in 2019, plans for the construction of the Ram Temple at the disputed site have been underway. The temple construction project is being overseen by the Shri Ram Janmabhoomi Teerth Kshetra Trust. The area has seen significant development efforts aimed at enhancing tourism and infrastructure to accommodate pilgrims and visitors. Ayodhya attracts devotees from across India and around the world, contributing to its ongoing importance in Hinduism and Indian culture.
